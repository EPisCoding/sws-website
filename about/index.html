About me

Hello, I'm Euan

I am a Mechanical Engineering graduate from Swansea University, currently transitioning into an MRes (Master of Research) in Mechanical Engineering, continuing the work I began in my final year dissertation on the Smart Walking Stick.

Background

Engineering foundations

I completed my BEng (Hons) Mechanical Engineering at Swansea University, graduating with First Class Honours. My undergraduate degree gave me a strong foundation across the full engineering spectrum, from fluid mechanics and structural analysis to electronics, embedded systems and product design.

Alongside my studies, I completed a year of industry experience at Ipeco, an aerospace and aviation manufacturing company, where I gained hands-on experience in a professional engineering environment working on aircraft seat components and manufacturing processes.

The dissertation that started it all

One project, six disciplines

For my final year dissertation, I chose to design and build the Smart Walking Stick. This wasn't just an academic exercise. It was a project I chose because it mattered personally, and one that pushed me across multiple engineering disciplines simultaneously.

Embedded systems

Programming a Raspberry Pi Zero 2W in Python to run the cueing, sensing and communication logic.

Electronics and PCB design

Designing custom circuits and a printed circuit board in EasyEDA to replace the breadboard prototype.

Mobile app development

Building a full Android application in React Native with Expo, from UI to voice control.

Bluetooth Low Energy

Implementing a full wireless communication stack between the Pi and the app.

Signal processing

Reading and interpreting real-time IMU sensor data from the six-axis motion sensor.

Human-centred design

Evaluating the prototype with real Parkinson's patients and building their feedback into the design.

The project was demonstrated live to a Parkinson's UK Southend support group in April 2026, and has since led to contact with Parkinson's UK Tech Partnerships and PD Buddy, a Parkinson's disease assistive technology business.

The MRes

Continuing as research

I am now continuing this work as an MRes at Swansea University, supervised by Dr Eifion Jewel. The research will focus on three areas:

  • Prototype 2 development: migrating to an ESP32, adding physical buttons, and refining the PCB.
  • Clinical testing: structured trials with Parkinson's patients measuring quantitative gait parameters.
  • Automated freeze detection: using machine learning on IMU sensor data to detect Freezing of Gait episodes in real time.
