## The Dissertation That Started It All

For my final year dissertation, I chose to design and build the **Smart Walking Stick** — an assistive device for people living with Parkinson's disease. This wasn't just an academic exercise. It was a project I chose because it mattered personally, and one that pushed me across multiple engineering disciplines simultaneously:

- **Embedded systems** — programming a Raspberry Pi Zero 2W in Python
- **Electronics and PCB design** — designing custom circuits and a printed circuit board in EasyEDA
- **Mobile app development** — building a full Android application in React Native with Expo
- **Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE)** — implementing a full wireless communication stack between the Pi and the app
- **Signal processing** — reading and interpreting real-time IMU sensor data
- **Human-centred design** — evaluating the prototype with real Parkinson's patients

The project was demonstrated live to a **Parkinson's UK Southend support group** in April 2026, and has since led to contact with **Parkinson's UK Tech Partnerships** and **PD Buddy**, a Parkinson's disease assistive technology business.

---

## The MRes

I am now continuing this work as an **MRes at Swansea University**, supervised by Dr Eifion Jewel. The research will focus on three areas:

- **Prototype 2 development** — migrating to an ESP32, adding physical buttons, and refining the PCB
- **Clinical testing** — structured trials with Parkinson's patients measuring quantitative gait parameters
- **Automated FOG detection** — using machine learning on IMU sensor data to detect Freezing of Gait episodes in real time
